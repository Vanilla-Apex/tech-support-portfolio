# 🛠️ Case Study: Thermal Alert Triggered by Improper Rack Airflow (Simulated Data Center Scenario)

---

## 🔧 Summary

This case study documents a simulated data center scenario in which a server triggered thermal alerts due to improper airflow management within a rack.

The objective was to identify airflow issues and apply corrective measures aligned with data center cooling best practices.

---

## 🔍 Scenario

A server reports elevated internal temperatures shortly after installation. The system remains operational but generates thermal warnings.

This scenario simulates an environmental issue caused by airflow obstruction rather than hardware malfunction.

---

## 🧪 Diagnostics / Validation Performed

### 1. Verified Airflow Orientation
- Confirmed server airflow direction (front-to-back)  
- Identified mismatch with rack airflow design  

### 2. Inspected Rack Layout
- Observed open rack spaces without blanking panels  
- Identified loose cables obstructing airflow  

### 3. Corrected Airflow Issues
- Installed blanking panels in unused rack spaces  
- Rerouted cables away from intake and exhaust paths  

### 4. Monitored Thermal Status
- Rechecked temperature readings after adjustments  
- Confirmed thermal alerts cleared  

---

## ✅ Outcome

Correcting airflow and cable management resolved the thermal alerts. The server maintained stable operating temperatures after remediation.

---

## 🧰 Tools Used

- Visual inspection  
- Rack airflow best-practice guidelines  
- Temperature monitoring indicators  

---

## 🧠 What I Learned / Explained

- Proper airflow is critical for server reliability  
- Empty rack spaces can disrupt cooling efficiency  
- Many thermal issues are environmental, not hardware-related
